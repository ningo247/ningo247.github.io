# 15-Minute Better-Than-Takeout Chow Mein

When you're craving Chinese food but don't want to wait for delivery, this 15-minute chow mein is your best friend. It’s savory, packed with crunchy vegetables, and uses simple ingredients you likely already have in your pantry.

## Why You’ll Love This Recipe

- **Fast:** Ready in less time than it takes to scroll through a delivery app.
- **Customizable:** Add chicken, shrimp, or tofu for extra protein.
- **Budget-Friendly:** Uses basic staples like cabbage, carrots, and soy sauce.

## Ingredients

- **Noodles:** 8 oz Chow Mein noodles (or thin spaghetti if you're in a pinch).
- **Vegetables:** 2 cups shredded cabbage (coleslaw mix works great), 1 shredded carrot, and 2 stalks of sliced celery.
- **The Sauce:** 2 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tbsp oyster sauce, 1 tsp sesame oil, and 1 tsp sugar.
- **Aromatics:** 2 cloves garlic (minced) and 2 green onions (chopped).

## Instructions

1. **Prepare the Noodles:** Cook your noodles according to the package directions. Drain and rinse with cold water to prevent them from sticking.
2. **Mix the Sauce:** In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, and sugar until the sugar dissolves.
3. **Stir-Fry the Veggies:** Heat one tablespoon of oil in a wok or large skillet over high heat. Add the garlic and vegetables. Stir-fry for about 2-3 minutes until the vegetables are slightly softened but still have a bite.
4. **Combine:** Add the cooked noodles and the sauce to the pan. Use tongs to toss everything together over high heat for 2 minutes until the noodles are evenly coated and heated through.
5. **Serve:** Garnish with chopped green onions and serve immediately. Enjoy your homemade feast!
