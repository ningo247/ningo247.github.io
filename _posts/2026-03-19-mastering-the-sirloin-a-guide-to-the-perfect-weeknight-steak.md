# Mastering the Sirloin: A Guide to the Perfect Weeknight Steak

Sirloin steak is the unsung hero of the butcher counter. It is leaner than a ribeye, more flavorful than a filet mignon, and significantly more affordable than both. Whether you are planning a casual Tuesday dinner or a weekend BBQ, the sirloin is a versatile cut that delivers every time.

## Why We Love Sirloin

Unlike tougher cuts of beef, the sirloin comes from the back of the cow, an area that doesn't do as much heavy lifting. This results in a cut that is naturally tender but maintains a robust, "beefy" flavor that true steak lovers crave. It takes well to marinades but is just as delicious with a simple salt-and-pepper crust.

## The Secret to a Perfect Sear

To get that restaurant-quality crust, follow these three rules:

1. **Dry the meat:** Use paper towels to pat the steak bone-dry before seasoning. Moisture is the enemy of a good sear.
2. **Room temperature:** Let the steak sit out for about 20-30 minutes before cooking to ensure even heat distribution.
3. **High heat:** Use a cast-iron skillet and get it screaming hot before the steak hits the pan.

## Quick Butter-Basted Sirloin Recipe

### Ingredients
- 2 Top Sirloin steaks (approx. 1-inch thick)
- Kosher salt and cracked black pepper
- 2 tablespoons high-smoke point oil (like grapeseed or avocado oil)
- 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
- 3 cloves garlic, smashed
- 2 sprigs of fresh rosemary or thyme

### Instructions

1. **Season:** Generously coat both sides of the steaks with salt and pepper. Don't be shy; much of the seasoning stays in the pan.
2. **Sear:** Heat oil in a cast-iron skillet over high heat. Once shimmering, add steaks. Sear for 3-4 minutes without moving them to develop a deep brown crust.
3. **Flip:** Flip the steaks. Immediately add the butter, smashed garlic, and herb sprigs to the pan.
4. **Baste:** As the butter melts and foams, tilt the pan slightly and use a large spoon to continually pour the flavored butter over the steaks for another 3 minutes for medium-rare.
5. **Rest:** Remove from the pan and let the meat rest on a cutting board for at least 5-10 minutes. This allows the juices to redistribute, ensuring every bite is moist.

Serve your perfectly cooked sirloin with a side of garlic mashed potatoes or a crisp wedge salad for the ultimate steakhouse experience at home!
