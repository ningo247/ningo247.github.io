# The Secret to Extra Crispy Oven-Baked Chicken Wings

There is nothing quite like a plate of perfectly crispy chicken wings. For years, the consensus was that you had to deep fry them to get that satisfying crunch. But what if I told you that you could achieve that same texture in your oven with significantly less mess and fat?

## The Magic Ingredient

The secret to crispy oven wings isn't just high heat—it's **baking powder**. When mixed with salt and coated on the skin, baking powder raises the pH level of the chicken, allowing the proteins to break down more efficiently and resulting in a blistered, crunchy exterior.

## Ingredients

*   2 lbs chicken wings (flats and drumettes separated)
*   1 tbsp aluminum-free baking powder
*   1/2 tsp salt
*   1/2 tsp garlic powder
*   1/4 tsp smoked paprika
*   Your favorite buffalo or BBQ sauce for tossing

## Instructions

1.  **Dry the Wings:** Use paper towels to pat the wings completely dry. Moisture is the enemy of crispiness.
2.  **Coat:** In a large bowl, toss the wings with baking powder, salt, garlic powder, and paprika until evenly coated.
3.  **Prep the Pan:** Line a baking sheet with foil and place a wire cooling rack on top. Spray the rack with non-stick spray to prevent sticking.
4.  **Arrange:** Place the wings on the rack in a single layer, ensuring they aren't touching so air can circulate around them.
5.  **Bake:** Bake at 425°F (220°C) for 45-50 minutes, flipping halfway through, until the skin is golden brown and crispy.
6.  **Sauce:** Remove from the oven and let rest for 5 minutes. Toss in your favorite sauce and serve immediately with celery and blue cheese dressing.

## Pro Tips
*   **Aluminum-Free:** Always use aluminum-free baking powder to avoid any metallic aftertaste.
*   **Don't Crowd the Pan:** Airflow is key. If the wings are too close together, they will steam instead of crisping up.
*   **Resting:** Letting the wings rest for a few minutes after baking helps the skin set so they stay crunchy even after you add the sauce.
