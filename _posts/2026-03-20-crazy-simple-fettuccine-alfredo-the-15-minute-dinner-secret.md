# Crazy Simple Fettuccine Alfredo

There is no need for jarred sauce when you can make a version that tastes ten times better in less time than it takes to boil the water. This Fettuccine Alfredo is the ultimate weeknight hack for busy families and comfort-food seekers alike.

### Why You'll Love This Recipe

*   **Minimal Ingredients:** You likely have everything in your fridge right now.
*   **Super Fast:** Ready in the time it takes to boil your noodles.
*   **Restaurant Quality:** Silky, buttery, and rich without the restaurant price tag.

### Ingredients

*   **1 lb** fettuccine noodles
*   **1/2 cup** unsalted butter
*   **1 cup** heavy cream
*   **1 1/2 cups** freshly grated Parmesan cheese
*   Salt and black pepper to taste
*   Optional: Chopped fresh parsley for garnish

### Instructions

1.  **Boil the Pasta:** Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the fettuccine according to the package instructions until it is *al dente*. **Important:** Reserve about half a cup of pasta water before draining.
2.  **Make the Sauce:** While the pasta cooks, melt the butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Pour in the heavy cream and let it simmer gently for about 3 to 5 minutes until it starts to thicken slightly.
3.  **Emulsify the Cheese:** Whisk in the Parmesan cheese slowly until the sauce is smooth and melted. If the sauce feels too thick, add a tablespoon or two of the reserved pasta water to loosen it up.
4.  **Toss and Serve:** Add the cooked noodles directly into the skillet. Use tongs to toss the pasta until every strand is coated in that velvety white sauce. Season with salt and pepper to your liking.
5.  **Garnish:** Serve immediately with a sprinkle of fresh parsley and an extra dusting of cheese.

### Pro Tip
For the best results, grate your own Parmesan cheese from a block. Pre-shredded cheese is often coated in cornstarch to prevent clumping, which can make your Alfredo sauce grainy!
